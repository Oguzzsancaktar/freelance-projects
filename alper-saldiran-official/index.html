About
Alper Saldiran Biography.
Early Years
They first appeared on the stage at the age of 5 while playing in kindergarten plays; immediately after, they became interested in folklore, especially Caucasian dances. Throughout elementary school, they continued both in theater and dance without interruption. After graduating from Tourism High School, Saldıran transferred to Marmara University's Tourism Department, but chose acting as a profession when the second year of school ended.
University Journey
During their university years, they portrayed the character of Yusuf in Turgut Özakman's play "Fehim Paşa Konağı," and won the award for Best Supporting Male Actor at the Inter-University Theater Competition with this role. Following that, they made their debut on television with the role of Burak in the series "Bir Dilim Aşk" (A Slice of Love).
Theater Performances
After leaving Marmara University, they started at Yeditepe University's Theater Department in 2005 with a scholarship. In the first year of their new school, they took part in a play called "Son Dünya" (The Last World) and participated in the Egypt Theater Festival, which included European and Mediterranean countries, as a student. The following year, they participated in the same program along with other theater students and performed in the play "Babil Kulesi" (Tower of Babel).
